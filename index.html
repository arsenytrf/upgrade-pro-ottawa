Our Specialty

Cabinet
process.

Eight steps, spray only, zero shortcuts. Two coats of primer and three coats of paint — every layer cured before the next one goes on.

  • 01

    Remove Doors

    Every door and drawer front comes off. Hardware gets labeled and bagged so nothing goes back in the wrong spot.

  • 02

    Degrease

    Full degrease to strip years of cooking residue. Paint won't bond to grease — this step is non-negotiable.

  • 03

    Sand & Smooth

    Hand-sanded and inspected for rough spots, dings, and grain raise. Surface has to be perfectly smooth before primer.

  • 04

    Dust Removal

    Every particle wiped and tacked off. Dust under primer means texture in the finish — we don't let that happen.

  • 05

    Prime — Two Coats

    Two full coats of bonding primer, sprayed for an even film. Each coat cured before the next goes on.

  • 06

    Paint — Three Coats

    Three sprayed finish coats of Benjamin Moore or Sherwin-Williams. Spray only — no brush marks, no roller texture.

  • 07

    Cure

    Proper cure time between every coat and after the final layer. Rushing this step is how cabinets chip in month one.

  • 08

    Reinstall

    Doors and hardware go back exactly where they came from. Final inspection with you before we call it done.

07 — FAQ

Straight
answers.

The stuff people ask us most. Don’t see yours? Pick up the phone — we’re happy to talk through it.

  • 01

    Do I need to move my furniture before you arrive?

    No — we handle it. Furniture gets moved to the middle of the room and fully wrapped in plastic. Floors get drop-clothed edge-to-edge. When we're done, everything goes back exactly where it was.
  • 02

    How many coats of paint do you do?

    Minimum two finish coats on walls and ceilings. Kitchen cabinets get two coats of primer and three sprayed finish coats — all cured properly between layers. Dark-to-light colour changes may need an extra coat — we'll tell you upfront in the quote.
  • 03

    How long does an interior paint job take?

    Rough ballpark: a single room is usually a day, a whole 3-bedroom home is 3–5 days, a full interior repaint runs closer to a week. Your written quote spells out the exact timeline before we start.
  • 04

    Do you stand behind your work?

    Yes. If something fails because of our workmanship — peeling, bleed-through, missed spot — tell us and we come back and fix it. No hoops, no extra invoice.
  • 05

    What paint brands do you use?

    Benjamin Moore and Sherwin-Williams. All low-VOC, safe for kids and pets. If you've got a specific paint or colour in mind, bring it — we'll work with it.
  • 06

    Can I get a quote without an on-site visit?

    We strongly prefer a walk-through — it's the only way to give you an accurate number and catch anything that'll affect the price. Free, takes about 20 minutes, no pressure to book.
